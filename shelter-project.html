Post-Disaster Temporary Shelter

The Temporary Shelter Project pioneered an efficient lightweight disaster shelter using modular fiberglass panels and a space frame that can be packaged compactly and rapidly deployed to provide resilient refuge in Guilan Province, Iran.

The Temporary Shelter Project pioneered an innovative rapidly deployable shelter optimized for disaster response scenarios.

Extensive on-site analysis, review of built precedents, and expert consultation informed the design. Key criteria included using lightweight, compact materials to enable portability and efficient assembly.

Based on recommendations from disaster management and municipal authorities, a 30 hectare site near Azad University of Rasht was selected adhering to emergency shelter siting guidelines.

The final design utilized a modular fiberglass cladding system, space frame structure, and insulation to provide a robust yet transportable refuge.

Developed in collaboration with my colleague Shadi Gharavi, this project advanced a practical sheltering solution for resilient communities.

The fiberglass sheets have pre-drilled holes with a diameter of 2 mm. Using specified screws, these sheets can be connected and locked together. The fiberglass sheets are then attached to the space frame structure using metal belt connections. The frame itself is connected via clamps at the joints.

The roofing sections are similarly connected and locked together through pre-drilled holes and screws. Beneath the roof sections, a layer of fiberglass insulation will be utilized to insulate the interior space from exterior temperature extremes.

In summary, the synergistic combination of design elements enabled realization of a next-generation rapidly deployable shelter optimized for structural integrity, ease of transport, and thermal comfort.
